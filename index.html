Waste Management Overview

Waste Management Trends at Northeastern

In the past four years, our data has highlighted a fascinating shift in waste management. We observed a significant drop in general waste in 2020, followed by a gradual uptick. Concurrently, recycling and composting rates initially fell but have been recovering steadily. These trends suggest the effective influence of initiatives by Northeastern’s Climate Justice & Sustainability Hub, including improved composting facilities and increased environmental awareness in our community.

NU Waste Management Disposition Over Time
Glickel, J. (2023). Waste & Recycling Summary. Boston; Northeastern University’s Climate Justice & Sustainability Hub.

Data from 2019 onwards indicates a challenge in waste reduction, with the majority not being recycled or composted. However, there's a noticeable rise in combined recycling and composting efforts, signifying our dedication to sustainability. The minimal waste diverted through donations or sales underscores areas for potential improvement in policies or infrastructure.

Analyzing Food Waste Patterns

Addressing food waste is crucial in our quest for sustainability. Our recent analysis across Northeastern’s various locations provides valuable insights into the generation patterns of food waste

Our analysis of food waste since January 2022 at Northeastern reveals expected trends and poses new questions. The IV and Stetson West loading docks, linked to dining services, emerge as the main contributors. Additionally, the variability at Speare and Curry’s significant contribution to food waste offer areas for targeted strategies.
